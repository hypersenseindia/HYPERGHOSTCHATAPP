What 3 Layers Actually Means.

Your message gets encrypted, then the key gets encrypted, then THAT key gets encrypted. Three separate AES-256-GCM operations. Three independent keys.

What The Server Sees

Firebase stores exactly two fields: msg and key. Both are base64 ciphertext. Neither can be decrypted without your device's crypto context.

What Your Device Does

Every message triggers three sequential AES-256-GCM operations. Server receives two opaque blobs. To reverse: need recipient's key AND sender's UID AND AES-GCM context.

HX-E2E Architecture

The 3 Layers, Broken Down.

Each layer uses independent AES-256-GCM. Breaking any single layer reveals nothing useful.

Layer 1

Message Encryption

Plaintext encrypted with fresh random 256-bit Key1.

Key1 → AES-GCM → Ciphertext
Layer 2

UID-Locked Key

Key1 encrypted using SHA-256(senderUID). Binds key to sender.

SHA-256(UID) → AES-GCM → LockedKey
Layer 3

Public Key Wrap

LockedKey encrypted with recipient's public key.

recipientPubKey → AES-GCM → FinalBlob

Decryption (Recipient)

Step 1: Unwrap FinalBlob with your public key → LockedKey.

Step 2: Unlock LockedKey using sender's UID → Key1.

Step 3: Decrypt ciphertext with Key1 → plaintext.

All three must succeed. Miss one = unreadable garbage.

What Each Broken Layer Reveals

Break Layer 3: LockedKey — still encrypted with sender's UID. Useless.

Break Layer 2: Key1 — but message is inside Layer 3 blob. Useless.

Break Layer 1: Ciphertext — but Key1 is locked behind two layers. Useless.

Each layer independently useless without the others.

Privacy Policy

We Collect Zero Data.

Data We Collect

Nothing. We do not collect, store, process, or have access to: your messages (encrypted before leaving your device), your message content or metadata, your IP address, your location, your device information, any analytics or tracking data, any advertising identifiers.

Data Stored On Your Device

Your browser's IndexedDB stores: decrypted messages (only while logged in), your session state. This data exists only on your device. We never access it. Clearing browser data removes it completely.

Data Stored On Firebase

Firebase stores only: your username and Firebase UID (public), your recovery email (secure, never shared), your public encryption key (required for E2E), encrypted message blobs (unreadable without your device). None of this can be used to read your messages.

Third Parties

Firebase Authentication — account management only. Firebase Realtime Database — encrypted message relay. Neither has access to plaintext messages.

Your Rights

Delete your account at any time — removes all your data. Export your messages at any time (JSON). Contact us with any questions.
